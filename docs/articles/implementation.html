Implementation

 

            I chose an outer room of 22 meters square.  The inner room is just two speakers, 2 meters apart (width wise).  I only use first order reflections; so, there are five delays.  One is for the direct source, and four are echoes off each wall.  The intensity decreases in proportion to the inverse square of the distance from the source (or reflection).

 

Figure 1 – Early Echo Response

 

 

            The next stage is the comb filter bank.  There are six of them in parallel.  The overall gain is g=g2/(1-g1).  For this implementation g=.83 and g2 is determined from g and figure 3.

 

Figure 2 – Comb Filter

Difference Equation: Y(n) – g1*Y(n-1) –g2*Y(n-(D+1) = X(n-D)  - g1*X(n-(D+1))

            Finally, the allpass filter is used to smear the echoes.  I used a 6 ms delay and a .7 loop gain for both allpass filters.  Afterwards, the early echo response and allpass output are scaled and summed to produce the final results.

 

Figure 4 – Allpass filter

Difference Equation: Y(n)-g*Y(n-D) = -g*X(n) +X(n-D)
